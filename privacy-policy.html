Privacy Policy

Add-in: Biweekly Activity Report · Last updated: June 2026

Summary: this add-in does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. It has no servers, no accounts, no analytics, and no third-party trackers. Your calendar and email data never leave the Microsoft 365 boundary.

What the add-in does

Biweekly Activity Report adds a button to the Outlook message ribbon. When you open the task pane and click Generate report, it reads your own calendar events and sent/received email over the date range you choose and builds a timesheet-style activity diary (meetings, emails sent, and estimated hours). You can copy the report or save it as a draft email in your own mailbox.

Data we collect

None. The add-in does not collect, log, or retain any information about you, your calendar, your email, or your usage. The report is built in your own Outlook session and is not transmitted to us.

How your data is handled

When you generate a report, the add-in signs you in (Microsoft nested app authentication) and uses Microsoft Graph — Microsoft's own Microsoft 365 service — to read your calendar and mail. The data travels only between your mailbox and Microsoft Graph and is processed entirely in your browser/Outlook client to assemble the report. It stays entirely within the Microsoft 365 boundary.

There is no PurposeBuilt Systems server in this process, and no third party receives your data. The add-in connects directly to Microsoft Graph from your own client; we never see, receive, store, or proxy your calendar or email. The add-in accesses only the signed-in user's own mailbox (delegated permissions) — never anyone else's.

The generated report and drafts

The report is held only in memory in your task pane while open. If you choose Save to Drafts, the add-in writes the report as a draft email in your own mailbox via Microsoft Graph — it is not sent anywhere and is not addressed to anyone unless you choose to send it. If you choose Copy, the report is placed on your own device clipboard. Nothing is uploaded to us.

Hosting

The add-in's program files (a small amount of HTML, JavaScript, and icon images) are served as static files from GitHub Pages. This host only delivers those program files to Outlook; it does not, and technically cannot, receive your calendar or email content back. Standard web-server access logs kept by the hosting provider may record routine request metadata (such as the file requested), but never any calendar or email content.

Cookies and tracking

The add-in sets no cookies and uses no analytics or advertising technologies.

Permissions

The add-in uses the Outlook read item permission and the Microsoft Graph Calendars.Read and Mail.ReadWrite delegated permissions. Calendars.Read lets it read your calendar events; Mail.ReadWrite lets it read your sent/received mail and (only if you ask) save the report as a draft in your mailbox. "Delegated" means the add-in acts only as the signed-in user and can reach only that user's own mailbox — never other people's data. No application-level or mailbox-wide access is requested.
