Add-in: Biweekly Activity Report

What it does

Adds a Biweekly Report button to the Outlook message ribbon. It reads your own calendar events and sent/received email over a date range and builds a timesheet-style activity diary — meetings, emails sent, and estimated hours per day — which you can copy or save as a draft email.

How to use it

  1. Open any message in Outlook and click Biweekly Report on the ribbon.
  2. In the task pane, set the date range and options, then click Generate report.
  3. Sign in / consent the first time (Microsoft prompt). The add-in only reads your own mailbox and calendar.
  4. Review the report. Use Copy to copy it, or Save to Drafts to create a draft email in your mailbox.

Options

  • Hours mode: Standard day credits a worked weekday at your standard length (default 8h), extended for overtime; Activity span uses the actual time from your first to last activity that day.
  • Standard day / Max day: the base length of a worked day and the cap on any single day.
  • Work start / end: your normal hours, used to detect overtime.
  • Count receive-only days: whether days you only received email (no sent email or meetings) count as worked.

Troubleshooting

  • Button doesn't appear: make sure you're on a Microsoft 365 / Exchange Online account viewing a message. Very old Outlook builds may not be supported.
  • Hours look off: they are estimates — adjust the Hours mode, standard-day length, or the receive-only toggle to match how you work, and always review before submitting a timesheet.
  • No meetings shown: the add-in reads your calendars; if your meetings are on a calendar you don't own or in a separate mailbox, they may not appear.

Privacy

Your calendar and email never leave Microsoft 365 — the report is built in your own Outlook session and we never receive your data. See the Privacy Policy.
